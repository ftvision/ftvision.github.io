I am a Ph.D. student studying human vision at Johns Hopkins University.

By Ph.D, I mean Positive, happy, and Dynamic, which should be pronounced as Pessimistic, hazardous, and Dreadful

As whimsical as I am, I follow my first principle of doing research: I do science for fun. Or precisely, I do science only when I feel happy.

I mainly use psychophysics to study visual cognition. In addition, I am interested in using computational models to reason algorithms used in mind, and using neuroimaing methods to reveal implementations in the brain, though I have done none of these recently. In fact, I am open to any method as long as the question is interesting. My random thoughts can be found in my Blog

Outside of research, I travel, enjoy outdoor activities, learn new languages, and watch animations. I also code and play with data when I feel like doing so.
